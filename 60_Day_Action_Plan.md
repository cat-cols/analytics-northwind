### Behavioral Prep (1-2 hours/week)

**Prepare STAR stories for:**

1. **Tell me about yourself**
```
"I've spent the last 2 years building data analysis skills through self-directed 
study. I became passionate about data when I realized how powerful insights can 
drive business decisions. I've focused on SQL, Python, and Tableau, and built 
a portfolio of projects analyzing real-world datasets. My Portland Airbnb analysis 
revealed that superhosts command an 18% premium while maintaining higher ratings. 
I'm excited to bring this analytical mindset to [Company]'s team."
```

2. **Why data analytics?**
```
"I love solving puzzles and finding patterns. Data analytics combines my 
analytical skills with business impact. When I analyzed Portland crime data, 
I discovered a 23% decrease in violent crime over 3 years - that kind of 
insight can inform policy decisions. I want to help companies make better 
decisions through data."
```

3. **Walk me through a project**
```
[Pick your SQL project - know it cold]
"I analyzed an e-commerce database with 10,000 transactions. I started by 
exploring the data to understand the schema, then wrote SQL queries to answer 
key business questions. My most interesting finding was that the top 20% of 
customers generated 67% of revenue, which suggests focusing retention efforts 
on high-value customers. I used window functions to calculate customer lifetime 
value and CTEs to segment customers by purchase behavior."
```

4. **How do you handle ambiguity?**
```
"In my Portland Airbnb project, the dataset had missing values and inconsistent 
formatting. I started by documenting all the issues, then prioritized which ones 
would most impact the analysis. For missing prices, I used median imputation 
within neighborhood groups. For inconsistent categories, I created a mapping 
table. I documented every decision in my code so the analysis is reproducible."
```

5. **Tell me about a time you made a mistake**
```
"In my crime analysis project, I initially forgot to account for population 
differences between neighborhoods, which made some areas look worse than they 
actually were. When I reviewed my results, something felt off. I went back, 
normalized the data by population, and the insights changed significantly. 
This taught me to always validate my assumptions and sanity-check results 
before presenting."
```

**Common Questions to Prepare:**
- Why are you interested in this company?
- What's your greatest strength/weakness?
- How do you stay current with data trends?
- Describe your ideal work environment
- Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
- Why should we hire you despite the employment gap?

## TYPES OF INTERVIEWS TO EXPECT

### 1. Phone Screen (30 min)
**Usually with recruiter**
- Tell me about yourself
- Why this company?
- Why data analytics?
- Salary expectations
- Availability

**Prepare:**
- 2-minute elevator pitch
- Know the company basics
- Have salary range ready ($55K-$70K for entry-level Portland)

---

### 2. Technical Assessment (1-2 hours)
**Take-home or live**

**SQL Assessment:**
- Write queries to answer business questions
- May be timed (60-90 min)
- Test database provided

**Tips:**
- Read questions carefully
- Test your queries
- Comment your code
- Explain your thinking

**Python Assessment:**
- Data cleaning task
- Analysis with pandas
- Create visualizations

**Tips:**
- Write clean, commented code
- Show your process
- Explain assumptions

**Dashboard Task:**
- Build dashboard from dataset
- Often in Tableau or Power BI
- Time limit: 2-4 hours

**Tips:**
- Focus on insights, not fancy design
- Include 5-7 key visualizations
- Add explanatory text

---

### 3. Technical Interview (45-60 min)
**Live problem-solving**

**Format:**
- Interviewer gives you problem
- You talk through approach
- Write SQL or Python live
- May use screen share

**Example:**
"Here's a table of customer transactions. Write a query to find customers who 
made purchases in January but not February."

**Tips:**
- Talk through your thinking
- Ask clarifying questions
- It's okay to look up syntax
- Explain your approach before coding

---

### 4. Case Study / Presentation (60-90 min)
**Some companies use this**

**Format:**
- Given dataset + business problem
- 3-7 days to analyze
- Present findings to panel

**Example:**
"Here's sales data for our product lines. Identify which products we should 
focus on and why."

**Tips:**
- Start with executive summary
- Use visualizations
- Make recommendations
- Be ready for questions

---

### 5. Final Round (2-4 hours)
**Meet the team**

**Usually includes:**
- Hiring manager (60 min)
- Team members (30 min each)
- Skip-level manager (30 min)
- Maybe peer interview

**Questions to expect:**
- Technical depth on your projects
- Behavioral (STAR stories)
- How you work on teams
- How you handle feedback
- Your career goals

**Questions to ask them:**
- What does success look like in first 90 days?
- What's the team's biggest challenge right now?
- What tools/tech stack does the team use?
- How is data work prioritized?
- What's your favorite part about working here?

## IF YOU'RE NOT GETTING RESPONSES

### After 30-40 applications with no responses:

**Troubleshoot:**

1. **Resume Issues:**
   - Get 3 people to review it
   - Run through ATS checker (Jobscan, Resume Worded)
   - Too long? (keep to 1 page)
   - Keywords missing?
   - Projects not prominent enough?

2. **Portfolio Issues:**
   - Are GitHub links working?
   - Is Tableau Public dashboard viewable?
   - Are READMEs clear and professional?
   - Do projects show business impact?

3. **Application Strategy:**
   - Applying to right level roles? (entry-level, junior)
   - Customizing cover letter?
   - Applying through right channels? (company site > job boards)
   - Following up after 1 week?

4. **Skills Gap:**
   - Do you actually know SQL well enough?
   - Can you explain your Python code?
   - Are projects too simple?
   - Need more advanced techniques?

**Adjust and iterate.**
